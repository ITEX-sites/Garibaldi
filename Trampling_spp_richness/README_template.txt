## ANAYLSIS NAME

Trampling Study Instructions for Garibaldi Provincial Park (Summer 2023)
Aims: This summer (2023), we are returning to re-survey each transect for species richness. We will also dig out the buried teabags and measure soil compaction with a soil penetrometer. We aim to quantify the continued effects of human trampling on these plant and microbial communities by returning each year, thus generating a multi year dataset. Such a dataset will allow us to answer how these plant communities are responding to continuous trampling and ambient warming. We will incorporate BC Parks data on visitation numbers in our analyses to answer how strongly yearly visitation rates correlate with community responses, or if these responses show more of a lag response to past visitation rates.

## Data collection notes summer 2022 - Who, when, where, why and how the data were collected
Explain any data collection information. 

Procedures:
Relocating Transects: Hike to general area of transects; Use GPS points on Gaia app as reference; Reference GPS points, 2022 transect photos, and 2022 photo bearing to find exact locations of transects. If stake is still in place: Place 10m transect tape according to the 2022 photo, starting from stake. If stake is missing: Find original slope and aspect at exact coordinate points, refer also to transect photos from previous season Place 10m transect tape accordingly. Place new, labeled stake at starting point. Place transect tape out from 0 m to 10 m, using flags to hold transect in place. Take new transect photo from starting point. Label clear laminated sheets with date, location (TM, BT, or PR), transect number, and bearing in the direction the photo is taken. Make sure that distinguishable landscape features are seen in picture. Compare new photo with photos from previous to ensure identical alignment, and use compass to shoot the same bearing as in original photo

Quantifying soil compaction: At every transect, measure compaction with soil penetrometer in as many quadrats as possible. Find area of exposed soil (no roots or rocks) in first quadrat to take measurement, move to next quadrat if not found. Take measurement with penetrometer and record the reading on transect-level datasheet. If penetrometer hits a root or rock while taking a measurement, take a new reading at a different location.  

Recording spp along transect: Set out 50 cm x 100 cm quads at every meter. Quadrat should be strung so that subquadrats are every 25 cm for 25 cm x 50 cm subquadrats. Record unique transect number and quadrat number. Identify plants within each quadrat. If cannot identify a species, take picture of species and indicate what transect-quad and any distinguishing features on the laminated sheet. Flag individual and ask other crew members for help in identifying the species. When the transect is completed, take a picture of the datasheet and put all these backups into the trip folder

Digging up tea bags to measure decomposition: Following this protocol (more info: Keuskamp et al. 2013 & Teabag Index) Find back the teabags from the previous year. Should be 15 cm uphill and 15 cm downhill of wooden stake and 8 cm deep. Carefully follow the string and dig up the tea using a spoon. Be mindful not to tear holes in the fabric. Write down the date. Leave tea bags to dry as much as possible on foil sheet in the sun.  Remove all soil parts and roots with a soft brush.  Sometimes soil parts are easier to remove after you have dried the bags for a little. If root in growth is very high, clean the bag, remove the tea from the bag, remove all roots. Store the old tea bags individually in paper bags. 
Label paper bags with sharpie according to tea type and transect number. Note on transect sheet the date of tea bag removal
